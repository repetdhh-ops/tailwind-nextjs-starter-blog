In the university's engineering faculty, Li Ang was engaged in a training project aimed at reducing urban pollution. This was not a theoretical exercise but a practical solution targeting the existing industrial emission problems. As the leading member of the team, he deeply understood the weight of this project. It concerned not just academic credits, but his future estate as an engineer.

The data from the first round of tests helped them identify a defect in an otherwise efficient function. To be exact, during the second session of the system's operation, the energy expense far exceeded expectations. Faced with this finding, the team's morale was low, and Li Ang could sense a distinct warning in the atmosphere. He leafed through the thick instruction manual, searching for the corresponding existing technical parameters. For him, this whole process was a grueling experience — the knowledge from textbooks was being put to the test, ready to examine his true ability.

Under the instruction of his advisor, Li Ang decided to expand the scope of the research. They faced many a restriction, but there was no other option. He had to establish a new methodology. It was then that a more experienced senior student offered a new version of the blueprint. This idea was not a pure creation out of nowhere; it emerged from a flash of inspiration during a late-night discussion. They adjusted the energy distribution portion, much like weaving a smooth narration where every link must follow a logical sequence.

The effect of the improved equipment was astonishing. It was not just theoretically effective; it represented a tangible improvement in efficiency. The once-missing data link was finally completed, and emissions dropped dramatically. At the project review session, the advisor looked at Li Ang approvingly, reading in his eyes a deeper comprehension of engineering ethics.

Li Ang was willing to believe that this success was not merely for earnings or credits. The project made him feel that his very reason to exist was closely tied to the environmental revolution society needed. He realized that environmental protection was not just a functional topic, but a profound responsibility. True wealth, he thought, refers not only to material estate, but also to the shared living environment of humanity.

Ultimately, they found the ultimate solution to the emissions problem and secured patent protection for it. This experience transformed Li Ang from a passive student into a warrior actively confronting environmental challenges. He compiled the details of the project and named it “A Solution for the City's Breath.” He knew this was only a small beginning. The road ahead was full of options, and every choice could alter the course of an event, but he was fully willing to face everything: to examine every suspicious piece of data, and to expand the boundaries of his understanding.

In his final speech, Li Ang said with emotion, “We cannot identify all risks, nor can we establish eternal truths. However, as long as we still exist and keep thinking, we can, within the existing framework, create an effective order. The important thing is never to lose the corresponding drive to act. That, in itself, is the genuine revolution of human civilization.”
